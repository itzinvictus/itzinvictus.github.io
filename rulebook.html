Tournament Overview

Battle of the Orgs is a premier esports tournament featuring organizational teams competing across multiple gaming titles. This tournament celebrates competitive gaming excellence while fostering community engagement and professional development within the esports ecosystem.

Tournament Objectives

  • Showcase top-tier competitive gameplay
  • Promote organizational pride and team unity
  • Provide professional development opportunities
  • Build lasting community connections
  • Establish new competitive benchmarks

Registration & Eligibility

Team Requirements

  • Team Size: 5-6 players per roster (5 starters + 1 substitute)
  • Organizational Affiliation: All players must be verified members of their representing organization
  • Age Requirement: All participants must be 16+ years of age
  • Geographic Restrictions: Open to all regions with stable internet connectivity

Registration Process

  1. Organization Registration: Official representative submits organization application
  2. Team Roster Submission: Complete roster with player details and verification
  3. Documentation: Provide required identification and eligibility proof
  4. Fee Payment: Submit registration fee (if applicable)
  5. Confirmation: Receive official tournament acceptance notification

Player Eligibility Criteria

  • Active member of representing organization for minimum 30 days
  • No current suspensions from other major esports competitions
  • Signed player agreement and code of conduct acknowledgment
  • Completed anti-cheat software verification

Tournament Format

Phase 1: Group Stage

  • Swiss System format with 4 rounds
  • All teams play minimum 4 matches
  • Best-of-3 series format
  • Top performers advance to playoffs

Phase 2: Playoffs

  • Single-elimination bracket
  • Best-of-5 series format
  • Seeding based on group stage performance
  • Winner-takes-all advancement

Phase 3: Grand Finals

  • Best-of-7 series
  • Extended preparation time between matches
  • Professional broadcast coverage
  • Live audience (venue permitting)

Tiebreaker Procedures

  1. Head-to-head record
  2. Map differential
  3. Round differential
  4. Head-to-head map differential
  5. Administrator decision

Match Rules & Regulations

Pre-Match Procedures

  • Check-in Time: Teams must check in 30 minutes before scheduled match
  • Roster Submission: Final roster submitted 15 minutes before match start
  • Technical Check: All players complete connectivity and software verification
  • Map Selection: Follow predetermined map pool and selection process

In-Game Rules

  • Pause Rules: Maximum 3 pauses per team, 5 minutes maximum duration
  • Substitution Rules: Substitutions only allowed between maps
  • Disconnection Protocol: 10-minute reconnection window, match continues if 4v5 is possible
  • Equipment Failure: Match pause permitted for hardware issues

Post-Match Procedures

  • Result Reporting: Winning team reports match results within 15 minutes
  • Demo/Replay Submission: Required for all matches (automated where possible)
  • Dispute Window: 24-hour period for match-related disputes

Map Pool & Selection

Current Map Pool: Maps will be game-specific and announced prior to tournament start with regular rotation to maintain competitive balance. Community input considered for future updates.

Technical Requirements

Minimum Hardware Requirements

  • Gaming PC capable of maintaining 144+ FPS
  • Stable internet connection (minimum 50 Mbps upload/download)
  • Gaming peripherals (mouse, keyboard, headset)
  • Webcam for player verification (playoffs and finals)

Recommended Specifications

  • High-refresh rate monitor (144Hz+)
  • Mechanical gaming keyboard
  • Gaming mouse with adjustable DPI
  • Professional gaming headset with noise cancellation

Software Requirements

  • Latest version of tournament game client
  • Approved anti-cheat software installation
  • Discord for team communication
  • Tournament platform client (to be specified)
  • Screen recording software (for evidence purposes)

Network & Connectivity

  • Wired internet connection strongly recommended
  • VPN usage prohibited during matches
  • Network testing required before tournament participation
  • Backup internet connection recommended

Conduct & Fair Play

Player Conduct Standards

Professional Behavior:

  • Respectful communication with opponents and officials
  • Punctual attendance for all scheduled activities
  • Appropriate language in all tournament communications
  • Positive representation of affiliated organization

Prohibited Behaviors:

  • Harassment, toxicity, or discriminatory language
  • Cheating, exploiting, or use of unauthorized software
  • Account sharing or identity misrepresentation
  • Match fixing or betting on tournament matches
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct or excessive celebration

Team Responsibilities

  • Ensuring all team members understand and follow rules
  • Maintaining professional team image and branding
  • Timely communication with tournament administrators
  • Proper equipment setup and technical preparedness

Organization Obligations

  • Supporting team with necessary resources and equipment
  • Ensuring player eligibility and documentation accuracy
  • Promoting positive tournament experience and values
  • Cooperating with tournament officials and investigations

Penalties & Sanctions

Warning System

Minor Infractions:

  • First offense: Official warning
  • Second offense: Map penalty
  • Third offense: Match penalty

Major Infractions:

  • Immediate sanctions ranging from match forfeiture to tournament disqualification
  • Potential suspension from future tournaments
  • Loss of prize eligibility

Specific Penalties

Technical Violations:

  • Late check-in: Warning (first offense), map penalty (subsequent)
  • Equipment failure causing delays: Warning
  • Unauthorized software: Immediate disqualification

Behavioral Violations:

  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: Warning to match penalty
  • Harassment or toxicity: Match penalty to tournament ban
  • Cheating or match fixing: Immediate permanent ban

Appeal Process

  • Written appeal within 48 hours of penalty notification
  • Administrative review within 72 hours
  • Final decision communicated within 96 hours
  • Appeals committee for major sanctions

Protests & Appeals

Protest Procedures

Eligible Protests:

  • Technical issues affecting match outcome
  • Rule interpretation disputes
  • Referee decision challenges
  • Player eligibility concerns

Protest Timeline:

  • Must be submitted within 2 hours of match completion
  • Include detailed description and supporting evidence
  • Pay protest fee (refunded if protest upheld)
  • Await administrative review and decision

Evidence Requirements

  • Screenshot or video proof of disputed incident
  • Witness testimony from neutral parties
  • Game replay files or demos
  • Timestamped documentation of events

Review Process

  1. Initial Review: Tournament administrator preliminary assessment
  2. Evidence Gathering: Collection of all relevant materials
  3. Committee Review: Panel of experienced officials and community members
  4. Decision: Binding ruling with detailed explanation
  5. Implementation: Immediate action on upheld protests
